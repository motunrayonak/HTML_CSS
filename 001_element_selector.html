THE SOLAR SYSTEM

MERCURY

Mercury is the closest planet to the sun and also the smallest in the solar system. It is a rocky planet with a surface covered in craters,much like the moon

Because mercury has almost no atmosphere, it cannot retain heat. This causes extreme temperatures, with very hot days and freezing cold nights.

It takes mercury just 88days to orbit the sun, making it the fastest planet. However, it rotate slowly, so one day on mercury is longer than it's year

VENUS

Venus is the second planet from the sun and is similar in size to Earth.

It has a thick,toxic atmosphere made mostly of carbon dioxide, which traps heat and makes it the hottest planet in the solar system.

Venus rotates very slowly and in the opposite direction of most planets. A day on venus is longer than it's year.

EARTH

Earth is the third planet from the sun and the only known planet to support life.

About 70% of earth surface is covered by water, and it has a protective atmosphere rich in oxygen and nitrogen.

Earth orbit the sun in 365 days and rotate once every 24hours, creating day and night.

MARS

Mars is the fourth planet from the sun and is known as the Red planet because of it's reddish, dusty surface.

It has thin air, mostly carbon dioxide, and temperatures are cold. Evidence suggest water once exited on mars.

Mars has two small moons and large volcanoes and valleys. A day on mars is about 24.6hours, and a year is 687 Earth days.

MARS PLANET
JUPITER

Jupiter is the fifth planet from the sun and the largest in the solar system. It has a gas giant made mostly of hydrogen and helium.

It has a great red spot,a giant storm, and dozens of moon, Including the four largest called the Galilean moons.

Jupiter has strong gravity and a fast rotation, completing one day in about 10 hours, while it orbits the sun in 12 Earth years.
